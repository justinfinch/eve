# Device Farms

Cloud services that let you drive a real physical device in a data center from your browser — proving the UX Adom needs, but today only for phones, leaving custom electronics as open white space ([source](../sources/adom-decoded-and-poc-plan.md)).

## Explanation

**AWS Device Farm** and **Firebase Test Lab** let you drive a real physical device in a data center from your browser. They validate the core UX of "remote-control a physical thing as a cloud service." But the embedded/IoT equivalent barely exists — people hand-roll Raspberry Pi rigs. A general-purpose, customer-facing **device farm for custom electronics is open white space**, and it is a chunk of what Adom is claiming ([source](../sources/adom-decoded-and-poc-plan.md)).

## Examples

- AWS Device Farm, Firebase Test Lab — real-device-in-the-cloud, but phone-centric ([source](../sources/adom-decoded-and-poc-plan.md)).
